# Contributor Covenant Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and leaders pledge to make participation in our
community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body
size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender
identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status,
nationality, personal appearance, race, caste, color, religion, or sexual
identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming,
diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment for our
community include:

* Demonstrating empathy and kindness toward other people
* Being respectful of differing opinions, viewpoints, and experiences
* Giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback
* Accepting responsibility and apologizing to those affected by our mistakes,
  and learning from the experience
* Focusing on what is best not just for us as individuals, but for the overall
  community

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

* The use of sexualized language or imagery, and sexual attention or advances of
  any kind
* Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks
* Public or private harassment
* Publishing others' private information, such as a physical or email address,
  without their explicit permission
* Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a
  professional setting

## Project-Specific Standards

In addition to the Contributor Covenant standards above, Open Paleo has the
following project-specific norms that reflect our commitment to scientific
integrity and neutrality.

### No Nomenclatural Advocacy

This project records the state of published taxonomy — it does not take sides
in naming disputes. Do not use contributions, issues, PRs, or discussions to
promote or suppress a particular name, author, or taxonomic opinion. If you
have a personal stake in a naming dispute, disclose it. Contributions that
appear to advocate for a specific nomenclatural position without scientific
basis will be declined.

### No Personal Attacks

Disagreements about taxonomy are welcome — they are a normal part of science.
Disagreements about people are not. Do not use issues, PRs, commit messages,
or discussions to disparage researchers, authors, or other contributors.
Critique ideas and evidence, not individuals.

### Scientific Integrity

All taxonomic data must be backed by published scientific literature. Do not
present personal opinions as established fact. Mark disputed taxa honestly
using `status: disputed` and document competing views with references.
Deliberate submission of fabricated data or references is grounds for an
immediate ban.

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Community leaders are responsible for clarifying and enforcing our standards of
acceptable behavior and will take appropriate and fair corrective action in
response to any behavior that they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive,
or harmful.

Community leaders have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject
comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that are
not aligned to this Code of Conduct, and will communicate reasons for moderation
decisions when appropriate.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all community spaces, and also applies when
an individual is officially representing the community in public spaces.
Examples of representing our community include using an official email address,
posting via an official social media account, or acting as an appointed
representative at an online or offline event.

## Enforcement

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be
reported to the community leaders responsible for enforcement at the
Open Paleo GitHub organization.

All complaints will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly.

All community leaders are obligated to respect the privacy and security of the
reporter of any incident.

## Enforcement Guidelines

Community leaders will follow these guidelines in determining the consequences
for any action they deem in violation of this Code of Conduct:

### 1. Comment

**Community Impact**: A minor or first-time violation, such as an error in
tone, a contribution that does not meet quality standards, or a mild breach
of the project-specific norms.

**Consequence**: A comment on the relevant issue or PR explaining the problem
and asking for correction. The contributor is expected to address the feedback.

### 2. Manual Review

**Community Impact**: Repeated violations, a pattern of poor-quality
contributions, or continued failure to follow project norms after being
corrected.

**Consequence**: The contributor's future issues and PRs are removed from
auto-merge eligibility and require manual review by a maintainer before merging.

### 3. Temporary or Permanent Ban

**Community Impact**: Serious violations, including harassment, deliberate
vandalism, spam, sustained pattern of disruptive behavior, or deliberate
submission of fabricated data.

**Consequence**: A temporary or permanent ban from contributing to the
repository, in accordance with the Contributor Covenant enforcement guidelines.

### Bans Apply to Contributions Only

The CC BY 4.0 license is irrevocable. A ban restricts the ability to submit
issues, pull requests, and comments to the repository. It does not restrict
the right to use, share, or adapt the published dataset. Anyone may use the
data regardless of their contribution status, as long as they comply with the
license terms.
